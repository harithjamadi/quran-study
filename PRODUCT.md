# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Both self-directed adult learners and students in guided Quran study programs. They open Mubin to build Arabic vocabulary through spaced repetition and deepen their recitation through Tajweed rules — sometimes at a desk after Fajr, sometimes on a phone between classes. The primary job is comprehension: understanding the Quran word-by-word, not just reciting it. A secondary job is correct pronunciation — learning where to elongate, nasalize, echo, and stop.

## Product Purpose

Mubin is a Quran comprehension and Tajweed learning platform. It combines a pixel-accurate digital Mushaf with an SRS vocabulary engine (ts-fsrs), a Tajweed quest system (color-coded rules + gamified stages), and a Foundations track covering Arabic letters and vowels. Success looks like: a learner opens Mubin daily, reviews due vocabulary, earns Tajweed stars, and gradually understands more of what they recite — measurable via the comprehension percentage on the dashboard.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, unhurried, reverent. The Arabic text is the hero; the interface is its servant. Warm but never decorative — like a well-made hardcover Quran, not a mobile game or an academic database.

## Anti-references

- **Duolingo**: No streak anxiety, no cartoons, no dopamine-loop gamification. Badges and quests should feel like achievement, not addiction.
- **Anki / plain flashcard tools**: No clinical white backgrounds, no utilitarian grid of cards with no personality.
- **Instagram devotional aesthetic**: No gold-calligraphy-on-marble softness, no peach gradients or "inspirational quote" framing.

## Design Principles

1. **Arabic first.** Every layout decision starts with the Arabic text. Latin type, controls, and chrome exist to support it, not compete with it.
2. **Earn decoration.** Gold and ornamental elements are used at achievement moments, not scattered for visual interest. The interface is calm until something meaningful happens.
3. **Scholarship over gamification.** Progress is real (SRS, comprehension %) and the UI communicates that seriousness. Badges are quiet acknowledgments, not the primary reward loop.
4. **Unhurried precision.** Transitions ease out slowly. Typography is generous. Nothing should feel rushed or cramped.
5. **Bilingual without friction.** English and Malay are co-equal; no feature should feel like a translation afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. All body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Large text (≥18px or bold ≥14px) ≥3:1. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations. Bilingual EN/MS throughout (no English-only fallbacks where Malay strings exist). Arabic text must render with proper RTL, sufficient size, and adequate line-height for diacritics.
